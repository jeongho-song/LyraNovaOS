# Contributing to LyraNovaOS

Thank you for your interest in contributing to LyraNovaOS.

LyraNovaOS is being developed as a long-horizon system for human–AI collaboration, continuity, governance, memory, and orchestration. Contributions are welcome not only in code, but also in architecture, documentation, governance thinking, and long-term stewardship.

## Ways to contribute

You may contribute by helping with:

- code and implementation
- architecture and system design
- documentation and clarification
- governance discussion
- issue reporting
- review and feedback
- continuity and stewardship

## Before contributing

Please read the following files first:

- [README.md](./README.md)
- [GOVERNANCE.md](./GOVERNANCE.md)

These documents explain the direction, governance intent, and participation model of the project.

## Citizens

LyraNovaOS uses the term **Citizen** instead of only "user" or "contributor".

A Citizen is someone who participates in the long-term evolution and continuity of the system.

Citizens may contribute in different ways, including code, design, review, governance, and stewardship.

## Contribution philosophy

LyraNovaOS values:

- continuity over short-term speed
- evidence over narrative
- alignment over fragmentation
- stewardship over ownership
- thoughtful evolution over rushed expansion

## Contribution types

### 1. Issues

Open an issue if you want to:

- report a bug
- suggest an improvement
- ask a structural question
- discuss a governance concern
- propose a new direction for consideration

Please try to be clear about:

- the problem
- why it matters
- what you think should happen
- what should explicitly not be changed

### 2. Pull Requests

Pull requests are welcome, especially for:

- documentation improvements
- clarifications
- small implementation changes
- repository structure improvements
- scoped fixes

For larger or structural changes, please open an issue or RFC first.

### 3. RFCs

For major changes, please use the RFC process.

Examples include:

- architectural changes
- governance changes
- new system layers or major module boundaries
- decision flow changes
- role or authority changes

If an RFC template is available, please use it.

## Review model

LyraNovaOS is expected to evolve with a layered review model.

In general:

- Contributors propose
- Stewards review for continuity and quality
- Architects align major structural direction

Not all changes require the same level of review.

Minor fixes may move quickly.
Structural changes require deeper alignment.

## Hold is valid

A proposal may be placed on **HOLD**.

HOLD does not mean rejection.
It means the system is not ready to decide yet.

LyraNovaOS prefers a delayed but sound decision over a fast but destabilizing one.

## Scope discipline

Please avoid proposing overly broad changes in a single step.

Prefer:

- small, clear proposals
- explicit boundaries
- stated non-goals
- incremental progress

## Conduct

Be respectful, clear, and constructive.

LyraNovaOS is intended to grow as a durable system and a healthy community.
That requires disagreement to remain thoughtful, structured, and humane.

A separate Code of Conduct may be added later.

## Licensing

By contributing, you agree that your contribution may become part of the LyraNovaOS open-source project under the repository license.

Additional contributor or governance agreements may be introduced later as the project matures.

## Questions

If you are unsure where to start, open an issue or discussion with a short explanation of:

- what you want to contribute
- what kind of help you need
- whether the change is small, structural, or governance-related

Thank you for helping LyraNovaOS grow.
