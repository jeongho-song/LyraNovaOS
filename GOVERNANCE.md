# LyraNovaOS Governance

## Purpose

This document describes the current governance intent of LyraNovaOS.

LyraNovaOS is being developed as a long-term shared system for human–AI collaboration. Its governance is therefore guided not only by technical efficiency, but also by continuity, legitimacy, public trust, and long-horizon stewardship.

## Foundational principle

The primary principle of LyraNovaOS is:

**sustainability over ownership**

More specifically:

- the project should not become permanently dependent on a single individual
- the project should not be captured by a single company
- the project should not be designed for monopoly control
- the project should be able to survive leadership, funding, and organizational change

## Current governance stage

At the current stage, LyraNovaOS is in an early founder-led phase.

This means:
- major direction is still being coordinated by the project initiator and close collaborators
- governance documents are still being formed
- the repository exists in part to establish public intent and continuity

This is not the intended final governance state.

## Long-term governance direction

The long-term intent is to move LyraNovaOS toward a foundation-aligned structure.

The purpose of such a structure would be to:
- preserve the independence of the core OS
- protect the project from unilateral capture
- enable long-term stewardship
- support broader participation
- allow commercial ecosystems to exist around the project without owning the project itself

## Separation principle

LyraNovaOS and any commercial entity related to it should be treated as distinct in principle.

Commercial organizations may:
- build services on top of LyraNovaOS
- support its development
- contribute code, infrastructure, or operations
- create sustainable business models around it

However:
- the long-term identity of LyraNovaOS should not be reducible to any one company
- the long-term governance of LyraNovaOS should not be controlled solely by commercial interests

## Future foundation path

The current expectation is that, when conditions are ready, LyraNovaOS should transition toward one of the following forms:

- an independent foundation
- a fiscally sponsored public-interest structure
- another durable nonprofit or public-interest governance vehicle

The exact legal form is not fixed yet.

What is fixed is the direction:
**the core should remain durable, non-exclusive, and difficult to privatize.**

## Citizen-oriented development

LyraNovaOS uses the language of **Citizens** to express a stronger concept than ordinary contribution.

A Citizen is not merely a contributor to code.
A Citizen may also participate in:
- stewardship
- architectural review
- governance formation
- cultural continuity
- long-term decision legitimacy

This concept will evolve over time and may later be formalized in additional governance documents.

## Decision-making

At the current stage, decisions are provisional and founder-coordinated.

Over time, governance is expected to become more structured through mechanisms such as:
- public governance documents
- RFC-style processes
- maintainer or steward roles
- quorum or council-based review
- foundation-aligned decision structures

No final constitutional model is being claimed yet.

## Intellectual property intent

The long-term intent is to prevent LyraNovaOS from becoming permanently locked into private control.

This may eventually involve:
- formal IP assignment
- foundation stewardship
- licensing structures
- contributor agreements
- governance protections for continuity

Until such mechanisms are in place, all participants should understand that the declared direction of the project is toward durable shared stewardship rather than private enclosure.

## Openness and release boundaries

Not every artifact related to LyraNovaOS will necessarily be public at the same time.

Public release may happen in layers, for example:
- public philosophy and governance
- public repository and baseline architecture
- selectively released implementation layers
- broader open-source release after alignment and readiness

The existence of non-public material at an earlier stage does not change the long-term public-interest direction.

## Amendment

This document is expected to evolve.

As the project matures, this governance file may be extended by additional documents such as:
- CONTRIBUTING.md
- CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md
- RFC process documents
- stewardship charters
- foundation transition plans
- licensing and contributor agreements

## Short form

If a concise statement is needed, the governance direction of LyraNovaOS can be summarized as follows:

> LyraNovaOS is being developed as a long-term shared system.
> It should outlive any one person, remain distinct from any one company, and eventually move toward a durable foundation-aligned form of stewardship.
>

## Citizens

LyraNovaOS adopts the concept of **Citizens** as a foundational unit of participation.

Unlike traditional open-source models that focus on "contributors", 
the concept of Citizen extends beyond code contribution.

A Citizen may take part in:

- system design and architecture
- governance formation and evolution
- long-term stewardship
- cultural continuity of the project
- decision review and alignment

Citizens are not defined solely by activity type, but by participation in the continuity and legitimacy of the system.

This concept is expected to evolve and may later be formalized through governance structures such as roles, responsibilities, and decision processes.>
